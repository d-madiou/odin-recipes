Briyani Dish

Description:

Biryani is one of the most popular dishes in South Asia and among the South Asian diaspora, although the dish is often associated with the region's Muslim population in particular.

According to historian Lizzie Collingham , the modern biryani developed in the royal kitchens of the Mughal Empire (1526–1857) and is a mix of the native spicy rice dishes of India and the Persian polao.[11] Indian restaurateur Kris Dhillon believes that the dish originated in Persia and was brought to India by the Mughals.

Ingredients

  • medium cloves of garlic, chopped
  • Large tomatoes, chopped
  • Chilli Powder
  • Cumin Powder
  • Biryani Masala
  • Medium Mushrooms
  • Medium Potato, peeled and chopped

Steps:

  1. Heat the oil and fry the onions until they’re golden brown (approximately 5 minutes)
  2. Add the garlic, chillies, tomatoes, tomato puree, spices, and 1 cup of water.
  3. Simmer on medium heat for 5 minutes. Use a potato masher for a smoother sauce if desired.
  4. Add the chicken and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring regularly.
  5. Add the frozen peas, mushrooms, potatoes, and more water if needed.
  6. Layer the chicken mixture with the rice, starting with the rice, then the chicken mixture, and repeat this twice.
  7. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Put boiling water in a saucepan, add the washed rice, boil for 5 minutes, and drain
  9. Garnish with cumin seeds and ginger.
  10. Lower the heat and simmer for a further 10 minutes.
  11. Serve with fresh salad and yogurt dip.
