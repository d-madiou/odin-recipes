Jolof Rice Dish

Description

Jollof (/dʒəˈlɒf/), or jollof rice, is a rice dish from West Africa. The dish is typically made with long-grain rice, tomatoes, chilies, onions, spices, and sometimes other vegetables and/or meat in a single pot, although its ingredients and preparation methods vary across different regions. The dish's origins are traced to Senegal.[3]

Jollof rice is one of the most common dishes in West Africa. There are several regional variations in name and ingredients;[1] for example, in Mali it is called zaamè in Bamanankan. The dish's most common name of Jollof derives from the name of the Wolof people,[10] though in Senegal, Mauritania and Gambia the dish is referred to in Wolof as ceebu jën or benachin. In French-speaking areas, it is called riz au gras. Despite the variations, the dish is "mutually intelligible"> across the regions and has become the best known African dish outside the continent.[2][11][12][13][14] It is found throughout the world wherever communities of West African immigrants have developed.[11]

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Cooking oil
  • Tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Chillies
  • Tomato paste
  • Bell pepper
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Thyme, Curry Powder, Grains of Selim and Bouillon cubes or Stock

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients
    • Choose the type of rice (long grain, short grain, broken, or parboiled).
    • Prepare cooking oil, tomatoes, onion, chilies, tomato paste, bell pepper, garlic, ginger, thyme, curry powder,
      grains of selim, and bouillon cubes or stock.
    • Prepare cooking oil, tomatoes, onion, chilies, tomato paste, bell pepper, garlic, ginger, thyme, curry powder, grains of selim, and bouillon cubes or stock.
    • Optionally, prepare additional vegetables like cabbage or carrots.
  2. Cook Protein
    • Cook chicken, turkey, beef, lamb, or fish separately until done. Set aside.
  3. Sauté Aromatics:
    • Heat oil in a large pot.
    • Sauté chopped onions until golden brown.
    • Add minced garlic, chopped chilies, bell pepper, and grated ginger.
    • Sauté until fragrant.
  4. Prepare sauce:
    • Add chopped tomatoes and tomato paste to the pot.
    • Add thyme, curry powder, grains of selim, and bouillon cubes or stock.
    • Simmer the mixture until it forms a thick sauce.
  5. Cook Rice:
    • Add the washed rice to the pot with the sauce. and stir to coat the rice with the sauce.
    • Add enough water or stock to cook the rice. and bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and cover the pot.
    • Simmer until the rice is cooked and has absorbed the sauce.
  6. Combine:
    • If using, mix in cooked protein (chicken, turkey, beef, lamb, or fish) and additional vegetables (cabbage or carrots) with the rice.
    • Stir well to combine all ingredients
  7. Final Cooking
    • Cover the pot and let the rice cook on low heat for a few more minutes to allow flavors to meld together.
